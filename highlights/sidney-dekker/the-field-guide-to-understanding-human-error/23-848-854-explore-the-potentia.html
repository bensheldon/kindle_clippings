The Field Guide to Understanding 'Human Error'

Explore the potential for restorative justice. Retributive justice focuses on the errors or violations of individuals. It suggests that if the error or violation (potentially) hurt someone, then the response should hurt as well. Others in the organization might have a desire to deny systemic causes, they might even fear being implicated in creating the conditions for the incident. Restorative justice, on the other hand, suggests that if the error or violation (potentially) hurt, then the response should heal.
