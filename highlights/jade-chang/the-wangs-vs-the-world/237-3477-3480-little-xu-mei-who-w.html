The Wangs vs. the World

Little Xu Mei, who worked in the university president’s office, had a single pearl-headed pin that she wore on the lapel of every shirt and dress. Barbra had been passing by the office when the pin broke loose and the pearl rolled into some unseen crack, never to be found again. She still remembered the tears and the consolation, the way that Xu Mei never stopped searching for that one tiny pearl. The Communists had it all wrong. It wasn’t the rich who were imprisoned by their possessions, it was the poor. — 237: 3477-3480
